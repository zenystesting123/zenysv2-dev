Frequently asked questions


1. What is a quotation?

A quotation is a proposal that holds details about the price of goods and services provided to the customer. A quotation is issued by a supplier and cannot be altered once accepted by the buyer.

2. What is the difference between an invoice and a quotation?

An invoice is a document that requests a payment to be done by the customer while a quotation is a document that contains the proposed price of goods and services provided to your customers.

3. Is a quote a fixed price?

Yes. A Quote is a fixed price. Once a quotation is accepted by the buyer, it cannot be altered by the seller. But in general practice, the buyer might negotiate on the price and terms and then finalize the sale.

4. What is the difference between quotation and tender?

A quotation is a proposed price of the services or products issued to a buyer by a seller. It gives a proposed cost of the services to the buyer/seller which is left to the buyer to accept or reject. While a tender is a process where sellers or service providers submit a proposal or bid to provide the goods/services to a potential customer. It is used to find out the best value for money from the service providers.

12. What is the difference between a quote and an estimate?

A quote and an estimate differ from each other in a few ways. A quotation is a fixed priced offer which means the service providers can move ahead with the work. Once approved, the service providers should adhere to the details mentioned in the quotation for the validity period. However, an estimate is an approximate guess of what the work cost. It is not binding and can change depending on the work.

15. How is a price quote different from a sales quote?

A price quote and a sales quote have only slight differences between them. A price quote is a legally binding document that gives a fixed price for a project. However, a sales quote is like a price quote but specifically gives cost details about the buyer’s products only. A price quote could include costs of either products or services or both while a sales quote gives the quantity and price for products only.
