An invoice is a document that provides details about the products and services a seller provides to the customer. Invoices are essential for businesses as they keep a record of the transactions and enable them to get paid for the products/services provided.

23. What is the difference between a bill and an invoice?

Both the terms are generally referred to as documents that provide details about the cost of products sold or services rendered to the buyer. An invoice is generally used by businesses that defines the payment requests to the buyer while a bill is a term used by a customer to refer to payments they owe to the sellers .

24. What is a bill of supply?

A bill of supply is a document issued by a GST registered business when GST is not applicable on a transaction or if he is a Composite Scheme dealer.

25. What is GSTIN?

GSTIN, known as GST Identification Number, is a fifteen digit unique number that is assigned to every GST registered person which will be State wise and PAN based.

26. What is the time limit to issue invoices in the GST system?

In case of services, the invoices should be issued before or after the services are rendered. If after, the time period of issuing an invoice is 30days. For goods , an invoice should be issued before or at the time of supply.

27. How many copies of invoices are required under the GST?

In case of goods, you require 3 copies, if it involves its transportation( for the customer, for your records and for the transporter of goods). For services, 2 copies of invoices are required.

28. What is place of supply under GST?

Place of supply is simply the place of delivery of goods or the location of the consumption of services.

29. What is reverse charge?

Reverse charge is a mechanism by which the buyer of goods/services is liable to pay taxes instead of the supplier.

30. What is mixed and composite supply?

A mixed supply means two or more individual goods/services are sold by a taxable person for a single price. Whereas, a composite supply means a supply made by a taxable person consisting of two or more goods/ services that are naturally bundled together.

31. How are interstate and intrastate transactions treated under the GST?

An intra state transaction is when the location of the supplier and place of supply are in the same State (including UTs with legislature) . In such transactions, the taxpayer is liable to pay State and Central Tax. The transaction is inter state, if the locations are in different states. Here, a combined tax called Integrated GST (IGST) will be collected by the Central Government.

What are the types of invoices?


There are different types of invoices based on different industries and different purposes. Say, you are a photographer. The invoice you create will not be the same as one created by an import business owner. Invoices vary and it is important you know about it. So,take a look at the 5 most commonly used invoices :

  1. Proforma invoice: A proforma invoice is more like an estimation or a quote. It is an invoice that is used to give the buyers a rough idea of what the products/services cost.This invoice is sent before a work is done and is non legal.
  2. Standard invoice : As the name suggests, standard invoice is a standard invoice format that consists of all the details that is to be included in any invoice( which includes the invoice number, invoice date, client details, billing information, list of items and its cost). This invoice can be used for different business transactions.
  3. Service invoice: Service invoices are mainly used by service based businesses like web designers, software developers, etc, who charge for their services based on the work time and not on quantity.
  4. Recurring invoice: Recurring invoices are invoices that are usually delivered at the end of each month. They are fixed rates and mainly apply to rental industries.
  5. Commercial invoice: Commercial invoicing also known as business invoicing is done by a seller(exporter) documenting any foreign trade such as shipment. Commercial invoices include the invoice details, invoice number,client details, item charges and shipment details as well.

1. What is an estimate in business?

An estimate is a document that gives an approximate price of goods and services provided by the seller to the buyer. A service provider can begin with an estimate to make an approximate guess of the cost of the work before issuing a quotation to the buyer.

2. How is an estimate different from a quotation?

An estimate is a document that holds an approximate price of the service provided or products sold to the customer. It is an estimated guess on how much the work cost. It is not binding and can change depending on the work. Whereas, a quotation is a fixed priced offer which means the service providers can move ahead with the work. Once approved, the service providers should adhere to the details mentioned in the quotation for the validity period.

What are the Different Types of Estimates?


  • Preliminary Estimate :

    • Also called an approximate estimate, this cost estimate is usually prepared in the initial stage to have an idea about the approximate cost of the project. They are prepared with reference to the cost of similar services done previously.

  • Quantity Estimate

    • This type of estimate is mainly used in construction projects in which the list of quantities for all materials needed for project completion are specified. This method of estimating helps the client get an idea about the list of quantities and the corresponding costs for each quantity.

  • Detailed Estimate

    • A preliminary estimate is what later becomes a detailed estimate when more details of the project are available. A detailed estimate is a more accurate estimate that could be prepared when there is detailed information about the quantity, costs, rates and all the materials required to complete the project.
