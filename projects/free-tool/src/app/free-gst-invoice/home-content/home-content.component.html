Frequently asked questions

1. What is an invoice?

An invoice is a document issued by a seller or a service provider which contains details about the product or the service sold to the customer. In simple terms, an invoice records the transactions between both the vendor and buyer and indicates what must be paid by the buyer according to the payment terms.

2. What is the purpose of an invoice?

An invoice acts as a record of the transactions made between the seller and the customer. It makes you stay organized and knowledgeable about which customers owe you and when the payment is due.

3. How is a receipt different from an invoice?

A receipt is a document against the settlement of payment while an invoice indicates the payment to be done by the buyer.

9. What is the difference between invoice and proforma invoice?

Unlike an invoice, a proforma invoice is an estimate of a product or a service a seller intends to sell. A proforma invoice is issued before order placement, while an invoice is issued before the payment.

10. What is the difference between quotation and estimate?

A quotation is a fixed price offer that can't be changed once accepted by the customer while an estimate is an approximate price that may change.

11. What is cess and where should I apply cess?

A cess is a form of tax charged by the government on tax with specific purposes till the time the government gets enough money for that purpose. It is imposed as an additional tax. CESS is mostly applied on the total amount, but you should check the details as per the case.

12. What is the difference between CGST and SGST?

CGST & SGST are part of GST. CGST stands for Central Goods and Service Tax and SGST stands for State Goods and Service Tax.

13. What is HSN code?

HSN code stands for “Harmonized System of Nomenclature”. It is a 6-digit uniform code that has been introduced to bring a uniform classification of goods.
