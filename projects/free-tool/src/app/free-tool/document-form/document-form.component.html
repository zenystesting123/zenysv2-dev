FAQ

  1. What is an invoice?

    An invoice is a document that provides details about the products and services a seller provides to the customer. It details the contact and billing information, gives an itemized list of products or services provided, provides the charges for the service/products sold and specifies payment terms if any. Invoices are essential for businesses as they keep a record of the transactions and enable them to get paid for the products/services provided.

  2. What is an invoice number?

  10. What is a tax invoice?

    An invoice created by a GST registered business for taxable products/services is called a tax invoice. Only if the recipient possesses this document, he/she can avail Input Tax Credit ( ITC). The tax invoice must contain details such as the product/service name and description along with the quantity, rate and tax charged.   13. What is HSN Code?

    HSN code stands for “Harmonized System of Nomenclature”. It is a 6-digit uniform code that has been introduced to bring a uniform classification of goods. HSN code facilitates the systematic classification of goods all over the world. This system assigns codes to products globally and simplifies the classification processes of products across the world facilitating international trade. This classification helps in understanding the tax rate of specific products in a country which applies to both imports and exports. Businesses with a turnover of more than ₹5 crores are required to furnish 6 digit HSN code and businesses with a turnover of up to 5 crores should furnish a four-digit HSN code on the B2B invoices. Using invoice generator you can add the HSN code in the respective field provided in the invoice template.

  15. What is the difference between a bill and an invoice?

    Both the terms are generally referred to as documents that provide details about the cost of products sold or services rendered to the buyer. An invoice is generally used by businesses that define the payment requests to the buyer while a bill is a term used by a customer to refer to payments they owe to the sellers . A bill, compared to an invoice, contains only limited details such as the price of the product/services, the quantity, rate and tax charged. It generally does not contain the name and contact information of the customer and any unique number. Bill serves just as a document for evidence of transaction, unlike invoices which is a legal document.

  16. What is a bill of supply?

    A GST registered business issues a tax invoice to the buyer mentioning the tax charges for the goods/services sold. However, some GST registered businesses cannot charge tax on the transaction. In this case, they should issue a bill of supply. A bill of supply is a document issued by a GST sregistered business when GST is not applicable on a transaction or if he is a Composite Scheme dealer.

  17. What is GSTIN?

    GSTIN, known as GST Identification Number, is a fifteen digit unique number that is assigned to every GST registered person which will be State-wise and PAN-based. It is mandatory to add the GSTIN on the invoices issued by a tax payer. While creating an invoice using Zenys you can add the GST number in the field provided in the template.

  18. What is the time limit to issue invoices in the GST system?

    The time limit to issue invoices will depend on whether the nature of supply is goods or services.
    In the case of goods, the invoice must be issued before or at the time of supply of goods. In the case of services, the invoice must be issued before or after the service is provided. If the invoice is issued after the service is provided, it has to be issued within the specified time of 30 days from the date of service provided.

  19. How many copies of invoices are required under the GST?

    In the case of goods, you require 3 copies, if it involves transportation. ( one copy for the customer, the 2nd copy for your records and the 3rd for the transporter of goods). For services, 2 copies of invoices are required. (one copy is issued to the customer, the other copy is retained by the supplies for their records)

  20. What is a place of supply under GST?

    Place of supply is simply the place of delivery of goods or the location of the consumption of services. The place of supply will decide whether the transaction is interstate or intrastate and if the taxpayer is liable to pay SGCT, CGST or IGST.

  21. What is reverse charge?

    Reverse charge is a mechanism by which the buyer of goods/services is liable to pay taxes instead of the supplier. If a service is listed under reverse charge the GST will be collected by the receiver of goods/services. This is applicable in 2 cases: When the supply of goods/services is notified by the government. When a GST registered person buys goods/services from an unregistered seller.

  22. What is mixed and composite supply?

    Supplies of two or more products/services can be mixed or composite supply. A mixed supply consist of two or more products/services that are sold for a single price. The products are not dependant on each other and can be sold separately too. Whereas, a composite supply means a supply made by a taxable person consisting of two or more goods/ services that are naturally bundled together. They are sold as a combination.

  23. How are interstate and intrastate transactions treated under the GST?

    An intra state transaction is when the location of the supplier and place of supply are in the same State (including UTs with legislature) . In such transactions, the taxpayer is liable to pay State and Central Tax. The transaction is inter state, if the locations are in different states. Here, a combined tax called Integrated GST (IGST) will be collected by the Central Government.

What are the types of invoices?

There are different types of invoices based on different industries and different purposes. Say, you are a photographer. The invoice you create will not be the same as one created by an import business owner. Invoices vary and it is important you know about them. So, take a look at the 5 most commonly used invoices :

  • Proforma invoice: A proforma invoice is more like an estimation or a quote. It is an invoice that is used to give the buyers a rough idea of what the products/services cost. This invoice is sent before a work is done and is non-legal.
  • Standard invoice : As the name suggests, standard invoice is a standard invoice format that consists of all the details that are to be included in any invoice( which includes the invoice number, invoice date, client details, billing information, list of items and its cost). This invoice can be used for different business transactions.
  • Service invoice: Service invoices are mainly used by service-based businesses like web designers, software developers, etc, who charge for their services based on the work time and not on quantity.
  • Recurring invoice: Recurring invoices are invoices that are usually delivered at the end of each month. They are fixed rates and mainly apply to rental industries.
  • Commercial invoice: Commercial invoicing also known as business invoicing is done by a seller(exporter) documenting any foreign trade such as shipment. Commercial invoices include the invoice details, invoice number, client details, item charges and shipment details as well.
